The Inputs the Code Looks For

To run the calculation, the code pulls from three specific inputs:

  • Birth Date (birthDate): Read from your Career Form to calculate your precise Full Retirement Age (FRA).
  • Claim Age (socialSecurityAge): Read from your Social Security Form to determine if your benefit will face an early-claiming reduction penalty or gain a delayed-claiming credit.
  • Earnings History Cache (this.earningsHistoryCache): This object maps your historical career earnings across calendar years to figure out your Average Indexed Monthly Earnings (AIME).

What Income Is the Code Looking At?

The script evaluates your annual gross earnings history from the year 1985 through 2026. However, the calculation does not look at your entire raw income unconditionally; it applies two strict filters to mirror actual federal rules:

  • Capped at the Maximum Capped Wage Base: For every single year evaluated in your data loop, the code automatically limits your earnings to the official IRS statutory maximum taxable wage base for that specific calendar year. For example, in the code loop, your raw pilot earnings for the year 2026 are capped at a maximum of $181,800 (any income you earn above that threshold is ignored, as it is exempt from Social Security payroll taxes).
  • The Highest 35 Years of Earnings: The engine indexes all of your capped earnings to account for national wage inflation over time. It then automatically sorts your historical calendar profile from highest to lowest, extracts only your top 35 highest-earning years, sums them together, and divides that total by 420 months to calculate your structural AIME base pool value.
